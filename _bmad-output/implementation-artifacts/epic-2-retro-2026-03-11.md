# Epic 2 Retrospective - AI Site Analysis Pipeline

## Epic Summary

- Epic: `Epic 2 - AI Site Analysis Pipeline`
- Stories completed: `5/5`
- Retrospective type: `Full retrospective after epic completion`
- Participants: Project Lead, Scrum Master, Product Owner, Senior Dev, QA, Junior Dev, Architect

Epic 2 established the full three-method analysis pipeline (`PATTERN_MATCH`, `CRAWL_CLASSIFY`, `NETWORK_INTERCEPT`) and unified result composition (`combineResults`) with shared confidence scoring. The site record now stores unified field mappings and confidence, enabling downstream review and extension workflows.

## What Went Well

- Strong incremental delivery: worker infrastructure in `2-1` enabled smooth layering of analysis methods in `2-2` through `2-4`, then proper unification in `2-5`.
- Architectural consistency held under complexity:
  - Analysis modules stayed isolated in `worker/analysis/`.
  - Shared logic was consolidated into `worker/lib/confidence.ts`.
  - API/data conventions remained consistent across stories.
- Resilience-first behavior was implemented across methods:
  - Methods return zero-confidence partials instead of crashing the pipeline.
  - Failure paths are explicit and logged with `[worker]` context.
- Domain adaptation improved quality:
  - Hebrew field and value heuristics were incorporated across methods.
  - Selector strategy clearly distinguishes DOM selectors vs JSON-path selectors.

## Challenges and Friction

- Manual runtime validation remained uneven in story artifacts:
  - Build/lint were consistently completed.
  - Several runtime verification checklist items remained not executed in-story.
- Complexity growth introduced maintenance risk:
  - Similar heuristics and parsing logic spread across three methods.
  - Selector and scoring behavior can drift without tighter shared contracts.
- Confidence-routing semantics had mixed wording in artifacts:
  - Some sources suggested threshold-based queue gating.
  - Final implementation routes to review with confidence metadata; this should stay explicit and documented.

## Deep Story Pattern Synthesis

### Common Success Patterns

- Early investment in worker/job lifecycle (`2-1`) reduced rework in later stories.
- Method-by-method decomposition improved change safety and traceability.
- Shared confidence extraction in `2-5` reduced duplication and clarified canonical scoring rules.

### Recurring Review and Quality Themes

- Positive: strong type discipline and explicit result interfaces improved reliability.
- Risk: manual checks still carry too much validation burden for behavior-heavy worker flows.
- Risk: method-specific heuristics are effective but not yet governed by a formal regression suite.

### Breakthrough Moments

- `2-3` crawl/classify introduced page-discovery behavior that significantly broadened coverage for non-standard sites.
- `2-4` network interception unlocked API-first sites where DOM-only approaches were weak.
- `2-5` combine logic transformed three separate signals into a practical unified mapping and confidence model.
